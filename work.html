January - October

ControlWorks telephony – Capita SSS

Aside from trying to wrap my head around some of the most awesomely deep and complex software I've ever seen, I've been taking a deep-dive into combined radio and telephony interfaces so I can make a start on improving some of the most fundamental interactions and features within CW - dealing with 'calls' for help, however they might come in to the control room.

The Hairforce products – The Hairforce

With the overall structure for the Hairforce sites in place I've been taking a closer look at The Hairforce's brand new product range. Although I can't say any more about the products themselves I've had great fun advising on the product branding and designing and liasing with a high-end London design agency on incorporating their design 'beats' into the site.

I've also been kept busy building a design system using Sketch (and Sketch Cloud) for The Hairforce brand assets and working out the best way to show this off.

Current role

In April 2019 I started work as a Senior User Experience designer at Capita Secure Software Solutions.

I'm currently working on the UX and UI for a whole range of complex features for Capita SSS's flagship software, ControlWorks. ControlWorks is software for emergency services control rooms.

Freelance and personal projects

I try to keep my hand in with a number of personal and freelance projects running concurrently with my day job.

I offer UI, UX and IA consultancy and support for long-standing Karyx and Nsource clients.

And before that…

Between 2014 and early 2019 I was a User Experience designer at the University of Bath.

I was part of the small agile team responsible for all aspects of design and front-end development across the main university website.

While at the uni I've done a fair few Show & Tell presentations.
Take a look at a selection on Slideshare.

And way back in the day…

I was making interactive CD-Roms for the BBC and Carlsberg-Tetley at MousePower in 1996, designing animated e-learning for BP at Nsource in 2000, and creating user-focused, content-driven websites for third sector organisations like the Ethical Trade Initiative and Refugee Action at Karyx until 2013.
