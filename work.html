So far this year…

A selection of highlights from 2018.

August - September

Content model mapping – University of Bath

Working through the remaining content types yet to be transitioned from Origins to Lens. Mapping out content structures, identifying potential new components and features and honing user stories for the product backlog.

Events content type – University of Bath

Working through the thorny issue of events, reviewing and prioritising user feedback from previous iterations and designing a layout that makes the whole event booking experience much smoother. Also, incorporating microdata at template level to give uni events more visibility on search engines.

Site-switching – The Hairforce

What happens when a user’s journey involves moving from one site to another? What’s the smoothest, easiest way to help someone slip seamlessly between the two. Add in some emotional stress and the frustration of a complete dead-end and this becomes a fascinating challenge.

May - June

Improving mobile navigation - University of Bath

The current implementation, a tiered, fixed navigation is *so* big it obscures almost all page content on smaller devices. This is obviously far from ideal. I’m in the thick of testing out nav prototypes built with InVision Studio.

Identifying user journeys - The Hairforce

Getting to the nitty-gritty of smoothing out the user flow for the upcoming new Hairforce site.

Where I am now

Since 2014 I’ve been a User Experience designer at the University of Bath.

During my time at the uni I've:

  • designed and built a bespoke publishing platform
  • developed Origins, our beta design language
  • redesigned the application journey
  • developed Lens, our new design system

Freelance and personal projects

I try to keep my hand in with a number of personal and freelance projects that run concurrently with my work at the University.

I mainly provide UI, UX and IA consultancy and services for some of my long-standing clients. My out-of-hours work with these great people help to keep me in touch with the latest developments and allow me close access to user groups I might not have spoken to.

And before that…

I was making interactive CD-Roms for the BBC and Carlsberg-Tetley at MousePower in 1996, designing animated e-learning for BP at Nsource in 2000, and creating user-focused, content-driven websites for third sector organisations like the Ethical Trade Initiative and Refugee Action at Karyx until 2013.
