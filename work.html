Designed and built a bespoke publishing platform

The University had outgrown its current CMS and 'off-the-shelf' options weren't quite right.

Challenges

  • Designing and building a bespoke CMS (‘Are you mad?’)
  • Defining and structuring a completely new publishing journey
  • Gaining staff and stakeholder buy-in

Solutions

  • Staff and stakeholder interviews/workshops to establish core needs and requirements
  • Iterative design process seeking feedback from users at each stage
  • Feature-led development cycle

Outcomes

More than three quarters of the content on bath.ac.uk is now created and managed through our Content Publisher application. Feedback from content creators, faculty web editors and marketing staff has been extremely positive.

Designed and built flexible front-end templates for multiple content types

The first step in our digital transition was to design and develop a series of flexible content type templates.

Challenges

  • No standard content structure - everything is bespoke!
  • No formalised digital design language in place
  • Organisational issues around change management

Solutions

  • Created prototypes and ran user workshops with content creators, editors and stakeholders
  • Developed content models and user journeys
  • Developed a simple content-led design system, Origins, inspired by Gov.uk approaches
  • Worked closely with the build team to develop robust, accessible, flexible templates in HTML. CSS and JS

Outcomes

We developed a clean, simple, content-driven design language that is flexible enough to hold almost any content thrown at it.

Developed Origins, our beta design language

Origins, our beta design system, was based on the content-led approach taken by gov.uk. It showcased a distinct move towards placing the page content front and centre, whilst paring back the design to leave just the essentials.

  • The first re-design of bath.ac.uk for almost 10 years
  • Stakeholder expectations
  • branding management

Solutions

  • A simplified, stripped back design system focused on clarity and readability
  • Designed to be modular from the ground up
  • Retained a degree of ‘classic’ University of Bath branding while introducing more modern elements

Redesigned the student admissions journey

One of the most important journeys on the University site, student admissions had not been re-worked for more than 7 years. We needed to start from scratch to meet the changing requirements of both internal and external users.

Challenges

  • Incredibly complex information hierarchy
  • Competing user needs (internal and external)
  • No unified approach to course structure around the University
  • Differing faculty/departmental requirements
  • A new look required

Solutions

  • Gathered user needs and requirements through stakeholder and end user interviews
  • Worked closely with dev and content teams to construct a robust course content model
  • Used guerrilla research to talk directly to the ‘difficult to reach’ 18-21 demographic
  • Designed an entirely new visual language for the course pages (that would become Lens)

Outcomes

Developed Lens, our new design system

During the course page redevelopment, it became clear that we were moving away from Origins in terms of look and feel.

Challenges

  • Creating a modern, flexible design system that can power 20 different content types, all with wildly varying content requirements
  • Establish a component-based workflow across all disciplines. We don’t design and build whole pages any more!
  • Move the University brand forwards without contradicting what we already have in print etc.
  • Establish a long-term design system that pulls together a style guide, a pattern library and other content creation

Solutions

  • Audit our existing design system Origins, along with other design assets across the University
  • Hold stakeholder and end user interviews to establish expectations, needs and requirements
  • Developed a new design language
  • Applied this new componentised design system to a number of content types
  • Created the Lens design system, pattern library and style guide. It’s not automated yet but it’s getting there!
  • Defined a new component-based workflow that integrates the pattern library into our process

Outcomes

  • The pattern library provides a quick and easy way to build static prototypes, reducing development time at this stage.
  • Lens has been embraced across the University with previously recalcitrant faculties and departments embracing transition more positively as the new templates better meet their requirements
  • Positive response from users in target 16 - 18 demographic. The new look, structure and approach is more in line with their expectations of a modern, dynamic organisation
